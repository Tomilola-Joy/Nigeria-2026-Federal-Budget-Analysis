# Nigeria 2026 Federal Budget Analysis
# Project Overview
A comprehensive data analytics project analyzing Nigeria's ₦51.59 trillion 2026 Federal Government Budget. This analysis focuses on three critical sectors, Security, Health, and Education to reveal spending patterns, resource allocation priorities, and their real-world implications for Nigerian citizens.

# Problem Statement
Nigeria's federal budget is complex and often misunderstood. Citizens struggle to understand how ₦51.59 trillion in public funds is distributed and spent. This project translates government appropriation data into actionable insights, making budget allocations transparent and meaningful to stakeholders.

# Data Source & Methodology
All data was extracted directly from the official 2026 Federal Government of Nigeria Appropriation Bill published by the Budget Office of the Federation (BOF). No projections or estimates were used only official government figures.

# Process
Manual extraction of budget data from BOF PDF into structured Excel workbooks
Data cleaning addressing inconsistencies in formatting, NaN values, and agency codes
Validation checks across all numerical columns and sector totals
Cross-referencing against source documents to ensure accuracy

# Key Findings
50% of the budget (₦25.88T) is committed to debt servicing and statutory transfers before any ministry operates
Security receives 2.4× more funding than Education and 2.7× more than Health
64% of operational spending goes to salaries and overhead, only 36% funds actual development projects
Teaching hospitals receive one-third of what their parent ministry headquarters costs to operate

# Deliverables
Interactive Power BI Dashboard: Visualize budget allocation across 56 MDAs, expenditure breakdown, and sector comparisons
Analytical Report: Comprehensive findings with business intelligence insights
Data Documentation: Complete record of cleaning decisions and methodology
Cleaned Dataset: Four Excel workbooks (Education, Health, Security, Federal Overview)

# Tools Used
Power BI – Dashboard visualization
Excel – Data extraction and cleaning
Python/AI Tools – Data processing and validation
